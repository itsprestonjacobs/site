Introduction

Thermal energy transfer occurs in three different ways: Conduction, Convection, and Radiation. Each of these methods plays an essential role in how heat moves through materials and across space.

Explore each method in detail to understand how they impact our daily lives, from weather patterns to the way we cook and stay warm.

Conduction

Conduction is the transfer of heat through direct contact between particles. It occurs in solids, especially metals, where particles are close together and can easily transfer energy to one another.

Fun Fact:

Did you know that metal is a much better conductor of heat than wood or plastic? That’s why metal pans heat up quickly, while wooden handles stay cool!

Convection

Convection is the transfer of heat through fluids (liquids or gases) where warmer areas of the fluid rise, and cooler areas sink, creating a circulation of heat.

Fun Fact:

Convection is responsible for the formation of wind. The warm air from the ground rises, and cooler air from above moves in to take its place.

Radiation

Radiation is the transfer of heat in the form of electromagnetic waves. Unlike conduction and convection, radiation doesn’t need a medium and can even occur in a vacuum (like space).

Fun Fact:

The warmth you feel from the sun is radiation, which travels through the vacuum of space and reaches Earth!
